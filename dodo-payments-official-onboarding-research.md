# Dodo Payments merchant onboarding and reapplication research

**Research date:** 10 July 2026
**Scope:** Current, first-party Dodo Payments documentation, policy, FAQ, and legal pages only. No account was accessed and nothing was submitted.

## Bottom line

- **Approval cannot be guaranteed.** Dodo says higher-risk categories are reviewed case by case, approval is not guaranteed, and it may decline or revoke access based on a holistic legal, financial, or reputational risk assessment. ([Merchant Acceptance Policy](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/merchant-acceptance))
- A previously declined merchant may create a new business account **only after fixing the reasons for rejection and only when doing so is not an attempt to bypass a risk decision**. Multiple accounts that leave the original issue unresolved will likely be rejected. ([FAQ, Q27–Q28](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/faq))
- A rejection can be appealed once. Dodo says the appeal decision is final; its Merchant Acceptance Policy likewise says enforcement reviews are one-time, although Dodo may allow corrective action or resubmission in some cases. ([FAQ, Q41–Q44](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/faq), [Merchant Acceptance Policy — Appeals & Resolution](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/merchant-acceptance#appeals-resolution))
- A document resubmission is not self-initiated at will: the verification team enables the dashboard's **Resubmit** option when it requests corrected documents. ([Account Verification — Resubmission of Documents](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/verification-process#resubmission-of-documents))
- **Marketplace payment facilitation / finder payouts are outside the standard MoR acceptance policy.** Dodo expressly rejects a marketplace or resale model where the merchant sells for others, operates a multi-vendor platform, or takes funds and forwards them elsewhere. ([Merchant Acceptance Policy — Businesses We Can't Support](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/merchant-acceptance#businesses-we-cant-support))

## Explicit official requirements

### 1. Business and location eligibility

- Dodo's preferred merchants sell useful, honest, legally compliant digital products that are easy to deliver. It specifically welcomes SaaS/AI products, digital goods, templates, plugins, and apps. The product must provide real post-purchase value and match what was promised. ([Merchant Acceptance Policy — Businesses We Love / What We Look For](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/merchant-acceptance))
- The merchant's KYC documents must be issued in a supported country, and payouts are available only in the listed countries. **India is currently listed as eligible.** The list can change for sanctions and regulatory reasons. ([Countries Eligible for Merchant Acceptance](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/accepted-countries-and-territories), last modified 8 July 2026)
- The prohibited list is expressly non-exhaustive. It includes adult/NSFW products, manual or in-person services, low-value or unfinished/pre-launch products, physical goods, illegal/age-restricted products, financial or regulated professional services, health/wellness, unverifiable claims, dating/social matching, gambling/gaming/virtual goods, crypto/NFTs, piracy or unauthorized resale, hosting/VPN/server products, telecom, scraping/spam, proxy or circumvention tools, cheating/manipulation, donations/fundraising, marketplaces/resale, ticketing/bookings, and weapons/violence-related content. The full definitions matter and should be checked directly. ([Merchant Acceptance Policy — Businesses We Can't Support](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/merchant-acceptance#businesses-we-cant-support))
- Categories that may receive enhanced review include AI generation, marketing/outreach, resume/hiring/exam tools, entertainment-only astrology, audio/music/chatbot generators, e-books, and standardized productised services. Dodo may request disclaimers, demo access, policy links, licences, or operating details. ([Merchant Acceptance Policy — Businesses We Might Support](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/merchant-acceptance#businesses-we-might-support))

### 2. Correct account type and ordered forms

- The merchant must choose **Individual** or **Registered Entity** based on the real present setup. An individual is a creator/sole proprietor whose payouts are personal; a registered entity is a legally registered company whose payouts go to its company bank account. ([Account Verification — Choosing Your Account Type](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/verification-process#choosing-your-account-type))
- Forms are sequential:
  - **Individual:** Product Information Form → Identity Verification (KYC) → Bank Verification.
  - **Registered Entity:** Product Information Form → KYC → Business Verification (KYB) → Bank Verification.
  - A UK registered entity must also provide W-8BEN-E. ([Account Verification — Required Forms by Account Type](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/verification-process#required-forms-by-account-type))
- The Product Information Form asks for the product website/landing page, one-sentence description, category, delivery method, degree of automation, sensitive or higher-risk areas, planned Dodo integration, product stage and customer acquisition, social profiles, and an accuracy/policy declaration. ([Account Verification — Product Information Form](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/verification-process#1-product-information-form))
- KYC requires a government-issued ID and a live selfie/liveness match. Dodo's FAQ says to use a physical ID rather than a screenshot or photocopy and capture a live image in adequate light. ([Account Verification — Identity Verification](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/verification-process#2-identity-verification), [FAQ, Q38](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/faq))
- A registered entity must provide its registration number and supporting incorporation document, details for all directors and ultimate beneficial owners, and government-issued IDs for all directors. Dodo defines a beneficial owner here as a natural person with at least 10% ownership/profit entitlement or other control. A Tax ID is optional in this KYB section if the merchant wants it on payout invoices. ([Account Verification — Business Verification](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/verification-process#3-business-verification-registered-entity-only))
- Bank details must match the verified individual or the registered entity's legal name. Dodo will not pay a mismatched or third-party account. ([Account Verification — Bank Verification](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/verification-process#4-bank-verification))

### 3. Website and disclosure consistency

- Dodo says a website is needed so it can understand and verify the product for live access. The Product Verification Form must match a website that is live, accessible, and reflects the same information. ([FAQ, Q5 and Q40](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/faq))
- The Master Service Agreement says the merchant must own and operate the submitted URL(s), use Dodo only on those URLs, and obtain approval for future supplier URLs. It also requires application/product/category information to remain accurate and up to date. ([Master Service Agreement, definitions and clauses 11–12](https://dodopayments.com/legal/terms-of-use/))
- Product details, live checkout, approved brand/domain/product line, and any gated fulfillment channel must remain consistent with the onboarding disclosure. ([Review & Monitoring Policy](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/review-monitoring-policy))

### 4. India-specific requirements

- India is on the current merchant/payout eligibility list. ([Countries Eligible for Merchant Acceptance](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/accepted-countries-and-territories))
- For an Indian individual, **PAN is mandatory** for KYC/tax purposes. A registered business may submit **Business PAN or GSTIN**; the FAQ says GST is optional unless the applicable registration threshold is exceeded or input credit is desired. ([FAQ, Q35](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/faq))
- A sole proprietor remains **Individual** even if they have GST under a trade name; “Organization” is for a registered legal entity. An Udyam/MSME certificate alone does not determine the type—the FAQ points to whether the bank account is personal or held by the registered company. ([FAQ, Q30 and Q36](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/faq))

## Rejection, resubmission, and reapplication

### Exact case: denied appeal for marketplace payment facilitation / finder payouts

- The stated reason directly matches Dodo's prohibited marketplace/resale definition: selling on behalf of others, running a multi-vendor platform, or taking funds and forwarding them elsewhere is not supported under its MoR structure. ([Merchant Acceptance Policy — Businesses We Can't Support](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/merchant-acceptance#businesses-we-cant-support))
- Dodo's FAQ says a rejected appeal is **final**; its policy says reviews are considered once and the appeal-review decision is final. The phrase allowing corrective action or resubmission "in some cases" is discretionary, not a right to reopen the form. ([FAQ, Q41–Q44](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/faq), [Merchant Acceptance Policy — Appeals & Resolution](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/merchant-acceptance#appeals-resolution))
- If **Submit** is disabled after the denied appeal, the public process offers no self-service override. Dodo must enable **Resubmit**. ([Account Verification — Resubmission of Documents](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/verification-process#resubmission-of-documents))
- Dodo does document affiliate integrations for tracking referrals and commission events for sales of the merchant's own product, via Affonso or Dub. That is distinct from operating a marketplace or using Dodo checkout as a pass-through payout rail. ([Affiliates](https://docs.dodopayments.com/features/affiliates))
- **Inference from the cited rules:** if the finder-payout model remains materially unchanged, a duplicate/new application would be an attempted bypass and is likely to be rejected. Rewording the same model is not remediation. A viable new application would require a genuine policy-compliant business-model change, accurate website/form disclosures, and preferably written confirmation from `compliance@dodopayments.com` before applying. Dodo alone decides whether the change is sufficient.

### Explicit process

1. Check the rejection reason in the Verification section of the dashboard; Dodo also says a rejection email is sent. If the reason is not visible, contact `compliance@dodopayments.com`. ([FAQ, Q3 and Q26](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/faq))
2. For correctable document problems, wait for the verification team to enable **Resubmit**, upload the specifically corrected documents, and click **Submit for Review**. ([Account Verification — How to Resubmit Documents](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/verification-process#how-to-resubmit-documents))
3. If the decision appears wrong, file the one permitted appeal through the dashboard. Dodo asks for a detailed explanation rather than a one-line appeal. Its FAQ says appeal review normally takes more than 48 working hours. ([FAQ, Q41–Q44](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/faq))
4. If the account was deboarded for material risk/compliance reasons, creating another account with the same identity or business details will likely be rejected. Dodo directs merchants who believe this was a mistake to email `compliance@dodopayments.com` with clear supporting evidence. ([FAQ, Q28](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/faq))
5. A fresh business account after a decline is permitted only after resolving the identified problem and only if it is not an attempt to evade the prior risk decision. ([FAQ, Q27](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/faq))

### Stated onboarding rejection / hold reasons

- Missing or incomplete identifying/business data.
- Documents that do not match the submitted personal or business data.
- Unreadable, illegible, or expired documents.
- A need for more evidence or clarification. ([Account Verification — When Can You Resubmit Documents?](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/verification-process#when-can-you-resubmit-documents))
- Incorrect entity type, mismatched KYC/bank/ownership names, or a third-party bank account. ([FAQ, Q24–Q25](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/faq), [Account Verification — Bank Verification](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/verification-process#4-bank-verification))
- A product that violates the Merchant Acceptance Policy, is incomplete, or is still under development. ([FAQ, Q44](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/faq))
- A website that is missing, inaccessible, inconsistent with the form, a placeholder/coming-soon page, or does not deliver immediate usable value. ([FAQ, Q5 and Q40](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/faq), [Merchant Acceptance Policy](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/merchant-acceptance))
- Location/KYC documentation outside supported merchant countries, or a sanctions/regulatory restriction. ([Countries Eligible for Merchant Acceptance](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/accepted-countries-and-territories))
- Legal, financial, reputational, fraud, or payment-partner risk even when the business is not named on the prohibited list. ([Merchant Acceptance Policy](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/merchant-acceptance))

### Stated review, suspension, or closure reasons

- Misclassifying the business/product, using proxy merchants/subaccounts/account takeovers, withholding essential onboarding information, or otherwise trying to bypass restrictions.
- Prohibited/deceptive/harmful products; gated or delivered content that does not match the disclosed product; suspected fraud or illegality; legal-authority or partner flags.
- Suspicious transaction volume; excessive refunds, disputes, or complaints; non-response to clarification, customer-protection, or fulfillment-access requests.
- New domains, product shifts, changed fulfillment channels, sanctions/card-network/legal issues, or use of unapproved brands/domains/product lines. ([Merchant Acceptance Policy — Enforcement](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/merchant-acceptance#enforcement), [Review & Monitoring Policy](https://docs.dodopayments.com/miscellaneous/review-monitoring-policy))
- Under the MSA, Dodo may suspend for missing/outdated due-diligence information or significant fraud/illegal-activity risk. It may terminate immediately if required by a payment system/provider, for suspected fraud/crime or legal noncompliance, or if monthly chargeback/cancellation/refund rate exceeds 0.5% of orders. Dormancy can also lead to deactivation. ([Master Service Agreement, clauses 10–11 and 17–18](https://dodopayments.com/legal/terms-of-use/), last updated 26 February 2026)

## Practical preflight — inference, not Dodo's promise or a separate official checklist

The following is an **inference assembled from the explicit rules above**. It can reduce avoidable errors but cannot ensure approval:

- Do not submit or open another account until the exact earlier rejection/deboarding reason is known.
- Fix that underlying issue and prepare evidence showing the correction; do not merely rewrite the application.
- Use the true entity type and the same legal name across sign-up, ID, registration, ownership, tax, and bank records.
- Submit current, legible originals/live captures; never use a screenshot, photocopy, expired document, third-party bank account, or someone else's identity unless that person is genuinely the owner and every ownership/KYC/bank record is consistently theirs.
- Make the website public, complete, functional, and factually identical to the Product Information Form. Show exactly what is bought, price/value, delivery method, and a working post-purchase experience; remove placeholders and unsupported claims.
- Disclose sensitive functionality, automation level, fulfillment channels, and business model honestly. If the product is near a restricted category, ask `compliance@dodopayments.com` before reapplying and provide demo access, policy links, licences, or disclaimers if relevant.
- If appealing, submit one complete, evidence-backed explanation because the official process allows only one appeal and its result is final.

## Important first-party inconsistencies

- The detailed Account Verification page says an Individual submits Product Information → KYC → Bank Verification, while FAQ Q6 says an unregistered business submits Identity and Business Verification. The detailed verification workflow and the forms actually shown in the dashboard should be followed; ask support if the dashboard differs.
- The detailed verification page says account type becomes fixed once a form is approved, failed, or on hold, while FAQ Q45 says support can enable resubmission to change Individual/Business “at any time.” Do not guess or create a duplicate account; use dashboard support/Intercom for the account-specific permitted path.
