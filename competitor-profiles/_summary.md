## Key Takeaways

- BountyBay’s strongest product advantage is not the posting form; it is the operational layer around the marketplace: verification, profile completion, messages, support tickets, and payout readiness.
- pleasefindmethis.com already had a strong protected-source/review schema. The main lag was the account and post-acceptance loop around the finder.
- This pass closes the most visible gaps without claiming automated transfers are live before provider approval: accepted sources now create payout cases, duplicate source attempts are reviewable, and staff can hold, process, record completed payouts, resolve source disputes, and update support tickets through an authenticated audit trail.
- BountyBay’s SEO footprint includes public bounty detail URLs and category pages; pleasefindmethis.com should continue building public solved-find and category pages.

## Gaps and Opportunities

- Keep trust language conservative: use funded request, protected source, payable reward, and review trail instead of escrow unless legal/payment setup supports escrow.
- Build actual payout-provider automation next; current readiness records, payout cases, and staff audit actions are the operational layer, not a live transfer rail.
- Extend admin actions beyond payout/support/dispute/duplicate cases into suspicious checkout attempts.
- Convert accepted-source and solved-request pages into public proof/SEO loops once moderation and privacy controls are ready.
