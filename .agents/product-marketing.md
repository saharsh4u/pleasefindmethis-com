# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: July 2, 2026*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** pleasefindmethis.com is the internet bounty board for impossible-to-find things.

**What it does:** Posters upload photos and details for a hard-to-find item, set a funded reward, and let finders submit protected source leads. Finders can earn by identifying real source links, seller leads, local handoff paths, or sourcing clues that meet the requester's requirements.

**Product category:** Hard-to-find item marketplace, finder bounty board, protected source marketplace.

**Product type:** Two-sided marketplace with user-generated requests, public bounty discovery, paid posting/funding, and finder submissions.

**Business model:** Marketplace service fee on funded requests. Current app copy indicates a 12% platform service fee, a 3% trust/payment protection fee, and a $10 minimum reward. Do not describe the payment flow as "escrow" unless legal/payment setup supports that wording.

## Target Audience
**Target companies:** Not primarily company-led. The first wedge is consumer and prosumer demand: collectors, families replacing sentimental items, repair-minded buyers, and people searching for exact discontinued goods.

**Decision-makers:** Individual poster funding the request; expert finder deciding whether a bounty is worth pursuing.

**Primary use case:** A person cannot find an exact item through normal search, marketplaces, Reddit, Discord, or local stores, and wants a protected way to reward someone for a valid source.

**Jobs to be done:**
- Post a clear bounty when normal search has failed.
- Protect a finder lead until the requester reviews it.
- Give expert finders a reason to act on niche knowledge.
- Convert scattered "help me find this" behavior into a structured marketplace workflow.

**Use cases:**
- Replace a lost childhood blanket, plush toy, mug, wall art, or family item.
- Source cult camera gear such as Ricoh GR, Fuji X100, Contax, Olympus Mju, Leica, Nikon F, and point-and-shoot film cameras.
- Find JDM watch references, bracelets, parts, and hard-to-search model names.
- Find discontinued repair parts, donor units, console shells, hinges, cables, battery covers, and appliance/electronics components.
- Turn comment-section "where can I buy this?" demand into a funded search.

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Exact-item search fails when the right clue, seller, collector, shop, local listing, model nickname, or old inventory is outside the searcher's own network.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Google and visual search return near matches, dead links, or generic listings.
- Reddit and Discord can help, but rewards are informal and often prohibited by community rules.
- Marketplaces are fragmented across eBay, Mercari, Etsy, Facebook Marketplace, local stores, Japan/proxy markets, estate sales, and forums.
- Paying strangers in DMs is risky for both sides.
- Finders have little incentive to reveal high-value leads publicly.

**What it costs them:** Hours of searching, missed listings, buying wrong items, scam exposure, and emotional frustration when the item is sentimental.

**Emotional tension:** "I know this exists somewhere, but I cannot reach the person who knows where it is."

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** General bounty/request boards and informal finder-fee arrangements. They fall short when source reveal, duplicate priority, payout logic, and requester review are not structured.

**Secondary:** Reddit communities such as r/HelpMeFind, r/wherecanibuythis, r/findfashion, r/photomarket, and niche repair/collector forums. They fall short when compensation, private source protection, and commercial mentions are disallowed or unsafe.

**Secondary:** Marketplaces such as eBay, Mercari, Etsy, Facebook Marketplace, Whatnot, Craigslist, Yahoo Japan, and Buyee/ZenMarket proxy flows. They fall short because users still need to know what to search and how to verify the right listing.

**Indirect:** Google Lens, image search, AI search, saved search alerts, personal shoppers, local resellers, and asking friends. They fall short when the item is not indexed, the description is unclear, or a human network is required.

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- Funded request before finder work begins.
- Protected source reveal instead of public lead leakage.
- Clear request brief with photos, must-haves, reward, location, and acceptance criteria.
- Marketplace structure for duplicate leads, disputes, refunds, and finder payout review.
- Public request and solved-find pages can become an SEO and social proof loop.

**How we do it differently:** The product does not sell inventory. It coordinates demand, source leads, and reward payout between posters and finders.

**Why that's better:** The requester reaches more niche expertise, and finders can monetize knowledge without buying inventory or exposing leads for free.

**Why customers choose us:** They need a safer and more structured path than DMs, screenshots, and comment threads.

## Objections
| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "Why not just post on Reddit?" | Reddit is useful for help, but many communities restrict rewards, DMs, affiliate links, and promotion. pleasefindmethis adds funded reward and protected source workflow. |
| "Can you guarantee someone finds it?" | No. Use "funded request" and "protected source review," not guaranteed-find language. |
| "Is this escrow?" | Avoid the term unless the legal/payment setup supports it. Say the source is recorded before reveal and the requester funds the offer before it goes live. |
| "What if the finder submits a fake or duplicate source?" | The product needs clear duplicate priority, rejection reasons, dispute handling, and proof requirements. |
| "Will this attract scams?" | Hard-to-find items attract scams. The marketing should emphasize source checking, public proof, seller verification, and avoiding off-platform DM payment promises. |

**Anti-persona:** People seeking illegal, unsafe, counterfeit, controlled, adult, stolen, privacy-invasive, or harassment-related items; anyone trying to bypass community rules; sellers trying to advertise inventory as if it is a bounty.

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** Search engines and marketplaces keep returning wrong products, dead links, sold-out listings, and scammy sellers.

**Pull:** A clear funded request can reach expert finders, collectors, local scouts, repair people, and niche communities.

**Habit:** People are used to asking Reddit, Discord, friends, or marketplace alerts for free.

**Anxiety:** Posters worry about losing money or attracting scams. Finders worry their source will be used without payout. Communities worry about spam.

## Customer Language
**How they describe the problem:**
- "Help me find this exact item."
- "Where can I buy this?"
- "I have searched everywhere."
- "This was discontinued."
- "I am willing to pay a finder's fee."
- "Google Lens only gives similar ones."
- "I need the exact same one, not a dupe."

**How they describe us:**
- "A bounty board for hard-to-find items."
- "A protected way to pay someone for a source."
- "A marketplace for internet sleuths."

**Words to use:** bounty, funded request, protected source, source reveal, exact item, hard-to-find, discontinued, finder payout, source lead, must-have details, accepted source.

**Words to avoid:** guaranteed, 100% safe, escrow, verified seller, official source, risk-free, instant, always, foolproof.

**Glossary:**
| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Poster | Person funding a request for an item. |
| Finder | Person submitting a source or lead. |
| Protected source | A source recorded before reveal so the finder lead is not exposed for free. |
| Funded request | A bounty where the poster has funded the offer before it goes live. |
| Accepted source | A submitted source the poster accepts under the request rules. |
| Solved find | A request with an accepted source or completed result. |
