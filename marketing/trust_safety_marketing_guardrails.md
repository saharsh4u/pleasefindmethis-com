# Trust And Safety Marketing Guardrails

This is operational marketing guidance, not legal advice.

## Product claims

- Describe pleasefindmethis.com as a free public request board.
- Say that anyone can add a public link, clue, seller path, compatibility note, or safety warning.
- Explain that users must verify third-party sellers and listings themselves.
- Explain that item purchases happen directly through the third-party seller's permitted process.
- Never imply that pleasefindmethis sells, authenticates, brokers, ships, or guarantees an item.
- Never claim guaranteed finds, guaranteed availability, verified sellers, or risk-free purchases.

## Public-content safety

- Do not post private personal information, credentials, account access, identity documents, or doxxing material.
- Do not publish phone numbers, home addresses, or private contact details without a lawful public business source.
- Remove spam, impersonation, threats, harassment, fraud attempts, and unsafe seller links.
- Encourage timestamped item photos and reputable third-party buyer-protection tools before a purchase.

## Prohibited requests

Remove requests or clues involving illegal, stolen, counterfeit, regulated, age-restricted, unsafe, rights-infringing, or prohibited goods or services. This includes controlled substances, weapons, stolen credentials, forged documents, malware, sanctioned trade, trafficking, illegal wildlife, and non-consensual personal-data searches.

## Third-party sellers

- Do not encourage people to bypass another site's rules or fees.
- Do not present a third-party listing as verified by pleasefindmethis.
- Encourage users to complete purchases through the seller platform's permitted checkout or contact process.
- Treat unsolicited private offers as untrusted until the seller provides specific, verifiable evidence.

## Community promotion

- Read community rules before posting.
- Disclose affiliation whenever the product is mentioned.
- Do not astroturf, invent testimonials, coordinate votes, or send unsolicited promotional messages.
- Lead with useful search advice; omit the product mention when promotion is not allowed.

## Review checklist

- Is the claim true on the live site today?
- Does it imply a guarantee or seller verification?
- Does it expose private information?
- Does it encourage prohibited goods or bypass a third party's rules?
- Does it accurately describe a free public request and public-clue workflow?
